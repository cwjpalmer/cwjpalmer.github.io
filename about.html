About

I am a cofounder at Minerva Foundry, where I work on projects that are sustainable, are transparent, and strive to benefit as many people as possible as much as possible.

I studied Engineering Chemisty at Queen's University. I have worked in the hydrogen fuel cell and biopharmaceutical industries and done applied research in nanomaterials, microbiology, and biotechnology. You can read about some of my work in the Journal of Visualized Experiments.

I lead the Queen's Fuel Cell Team while it began work on the world's first fuel cell snowmobile. I developed from 10 to 60 people to drive this new project forward. I simultaneously lead the Queen's iGEM team, which engineers organisms to perform new functions. I then founded the Queen's Synthetic Biology Organization.

I love learning new things and exploring new opportunities. I'd love to meet you!

You can find me on twitter, Linkedin, or read my posts on the Minerva Foundry blog
